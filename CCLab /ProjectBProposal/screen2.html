Olives were first cultivated around 7,000 years ago in Mediterranean regions. Their botanical name being Olea europaea, which means 'European olive' comes from the Oleaceae family. The oleaceae family is named for its importance in olive trees and shrubs. People know olives for the renowned condiment, olive oil, and some people put olives on their salads.
